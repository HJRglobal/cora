### Cross-Line Insights

- **Energy and Pure use identical flavor-to-color mapping.** Pure is a visual refresh only. Critical to communicate clearly when briefing BDM or any printer to avoid re-separation errors.
- **PANTONE 663 C is Pure Line-exclusive.** It does not appear in Energy or Mood files in any capacity. It is the single physical ink difference between Energy and Pure at the can level.
- **Mood is a parallel palette, not additive.** The 6 Mood-specific inks do not include the core 5 Energy colors — the two lines run in parallel. Do not assume Mood shares Energy inks.
- **PANTONE 1505 C** is the bridge color between Mood (Orange primary) and SOJO (structural ink). It does not appear in F3 Energy or Pure Line.
- **PANTONE 178 C vs. PANTONE 1787 C** — two distinct inks, both present in Energy can artwork. Neither is a typo of the other. 178 C = coral salmon (accent, Energy can only). 1787 C = Strawberry Lemonade co-primary (tray + can).
- **PANTONE 177 C vs. PANTONE 178 C** — also distinct. 177 C = Mood-only gradient companion. 178 C = Energy-only accent. They do not cross lines.
- Black and White are universal structural inks across all lines and formats. Not counted as flavor spot colors.
- "Aluminum" swatch in can dieline files = bare can substrate reference. Not a printed ink.

### Production & Workflow Notes

- All PMS data extracted directly from embedded XMP swatch metadata and PostScript custom color declarations — exact specified print colors, not visual approximations.
- The `%%DocumentCustomColors` PostScript field in each tray PDF identifies that flavor's primary hero ink.
- The Ball Corporation color spec table at the bottom of each can dieline is the authoritative per-SKU ink call-out for the can printer.
- AutoCAD Color entries in AI dieline files are structural dieline layers, not print inks. Disregard for color spec purposes.
- "crazy white" and "Die Color" designations in Ball dieline files are structural references, not branded color names.
- **BDM (Larry Stone / Big D Media) executes to spec on color — they do not own or decide color assignments.** All PMS decisions originate from Harrison Rogers and the internal marketing team.
